Approaching Prospective Clients

Making first contact with a prospective client can be difficult and nerve-wracking. Here are a few points to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Be friendly!
  • Get to the point – “I saw you all recently got a building permit for plumbing”
  • Give your work history - “I’ve done some plumbing.”
  • Sell your strengths - “I’m a very hard worker.”
  • Give them your info – “Here’s my cell number”

Don't:

  • Be nervous – “I just wanted to” “I thought that maybe”
  • Lie about skills or experience
  • Forget to leave your information!

Don't Know What to Say?

If you don't have a rehearsed script to say, you can use this. Fit it to your own situation.

Hi, my name is _______. What do you all need help with? I can do _____, _________, and _______. Here’s my contact information. I can start work on _________.
